# Staff Guides 101

This handout is for DWDS staff who use the system for daily water utility operations. Study this guide first before handling live customer records, billing work, or payment posting.

## What DWDS Is For

DWDS helps staff manage the full office workflow:
- customer records
- meter assignment and monitoring
- tariff setup
- meter reading intake and approval
- bill generation and printing
- payment posting
- collections and overdue follow-up
- staff access control

## Core Rule

Always work from accurate records. If customer details, meter data, readings, bills, or payments are entered incorrectly, the error will affect every step after it.

## Standard Daily Workflow

Use this order whenever possible:
1. Confirm customer record.
2. Confirm meter assignment.
3. Enter or review meter reading.
4. Approve reading if correct.
5. Generate bill from approved reading.
6. Print or distribute the bill as needed.
7. Post payment only after receiving valid payment details.
8. Review unpaid and overdue accounts for follow-up.

## Main Staff Modules

### 1. Dashboard
- Use the dashboard to see the current work situation.
- Check totals, pending work, and module shortcuts before starting tasks.
- Review alerts or backlogs that need same-day action.

### 2. Customers
- Create and update customer records.
- Verify account number, full name, address, contact number, and email.
- Make sure the customer record is correct before linking a meter.
- Do not create duplicate customer records.

### 3. Meters
- Assign meters to the correct customer account.
- Check the meter number carefully before saving.
- Update meter details only when a verified service change happens.
- If a meter is transferred, make sure the effective date is correct.

### 4. Routes
- Use route records to organize field operations.
- Confirm that meters are assigned to the proper route or area when required.

### 5. Readings
- Enter meter readings clearly and carefully.
- Review previous and current values before approval.
- Do not approve doubtful or incomplete readings.
- If there is a reading issue, escalate it before billing.

### 6. Tariffs
- Tariffs control how bills are computed.
- Only authorized staff should change tariff settings.
- Never edit tariff rules without approval because billing results depend on them.

### 7. Billing
- Bills are generated from approved readings.
- Check the billing period, meter, customer, and amount before finalizing work.
- Use the billing page to manage unpaid bills and printing activity.
- Reopening or regenerating billing cycles must be handled carefully and only by authorized staff.

### 8. Payments
- Post payments only against the correct bill and customer account.
- Confirm payment amount, date, method, and reference number.
- Do not guess missing payment information.
- Once a payment is posted, review it immediately for errors.

### 9. Collections
- Use collections to monitor paid and unpaid accounts.
- Review outstanding balances and recent payment activity.
- Keep collection records clean so follow-up actions are based on correct balances.

### 10. Follow-Up
- Use follow-up for overdue or unpaid accounts.
- Record notices, reminders, and collection actions properly.
- Keep internal notes professional and factual.

### 11. Staff Access
- Staff access is for creating and managing internal user accounts.
- Access must match the employee's role and responsibility.
- Do not share passwords or leave accounts active for former staff.

### 12. System Readiness
- Use this area to confirm whether important parts of the system are ready for staff use.
- Check key screens before reopening operations after maintenance or updates.

## Study Reminders For Staff

- Encode carefully and review before saving.
- Search first before creating a new record.
- Protect customer and company information.
- Use only your assigned account.
- Report system issues immediately.
- Follow approval rules for sensitive actions.
- Keep printed records and digital records aligned.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- creating duplicate customer accounts
- linking the wrong meter to a customer
- approving incorrect readings
- generating bills before checking tariff and reading accuracy
- posting a payment to the wrong bill
- changing records without authority
- skipping follow-up for overdue balances

## Quick Practice Checklist

Before starting live work, each staff member should be able to:
- sign in successfully
- locate the dashboard
- find a customer account
- confirm a linked meter
- identify an approved reading
- locate a bill record
- identify where payments are posted
- identify where overdue follow-up is tracked

## Final Reminder

DWDS is a staff operations system. Every action inside the system can affect customers, billing accuracy, and collection performance. Work slowly, verify details, and ask for approval when a task affects billing, payments, or staff access.
